Process

How MpowerNPO
gets things done

A structured, six-step model that turns real nonprofit IT needs into working solutions — responsibly and measurably.

01
📥

Nonprofit Submits a Request

A nonprofit identifies a specific IT or process pain point and submits a request for review. Concrete problems only — no vague asks.

02
🤖

AI Guides Requirements Clarification

Our AI assistant works through the IT need with the nonprofit to generate a clear, structured requirements document.

03
✅

Scope Reviewed & Approved

Before any code is written, a mentor reviews and approves the project scope to ensure feasibility and safety.

04
👥

Youth Team Assigned

A trained student team is matched to the request based on skills, availability, and project complexity.

05
🧭

Mentor-Overseen Execution

An experienced mentor oversees every stage of development, ensuring ethical standards and quality delivery.

06
📊

Delivery & Documented Impact

The solution is implemented and measurable outcomes are documented — so every project shows its real community value.

What We Build

Practical IT solutions
for real needs

MpowerNPO focuses on high-impact IT improvements — not experimental tech for its own sake.

🙋

Volunteer Registration Automation

Replacing manual sign-up sheets with digital workflows can save nonprofit staff 16+ hours per week — time better spent directly on their mission.

📋

Digitizing Intake Workflows

Paper-based intake systems slow service delivery and create compliance risk. We build accessible digital intake tools tailored to each nonprofit's unique IT needs.

🗂️

Data Organization for Reporting

42% of nonprofits cite lack of adequate data systems as a top challenge. We organize existing data so it's grant-ready, board-ready, and impact-measurable.

📈

Service Tracking Dashboards

Digitally mature nonprofits are 4× more likely to meet their mission goals. Our dashboards give staff a live view of programs — no technical expertise needed.

Why It Matters — Iowa

The IT gap is right here
in Iowa

Iowa has one of the strongest nonprofit ecosystems in the Midwest — but most organizations still struggle with outdated IT and manual processes.

32,836
Nonprofits in Iowa

Iowa is home to over 32,000 registered nonprofits — including 18,671 charitable 501(c)(3)s — employing nearly 239,000 people statewide.

42%
List Digital Transformation as a Top Priority

42% of nonprofit leaders cite digital transformation as a top-3 goal — but lack the technical support to act on it. (BDO Nonprofit Survey)

16 hrs
Lost to Manual Admin Work Per Week

Nonprofit staff lose an average of 16 hours every week to manual administrative tasks — the equivalent of two full workdays that could serve the community.

4×
Mission Success for Tech-Enabled Orgs

Salesforce research shows nonprofits that are "digitally mature" are four times more likely to meet their mission goals than those using manual systems.

59%
Ready to Invest — But Need Guidance

59% of nonprofits are prepared to increase technology spending — but without structured support, most don't know where to start. That's where MpowerNPO steps in.

💡
The opportunity is clear. Iowa has thousands of nonprofits doing critical work — and an entire generation of students with coding skills who've never applied them to a real community IT need. MpowerNPO bridges that gap, one request at a time.
Meet the Founder

Mayank Bhatt

Founder & Youth Leader · MpowerNPO · Johnston Middle School, 9th Grade

Mayank started MpowerNPO with a straightforward observation: students were learning to code in classrooms, and nonprofits were struggling with the same IT problems year after year — and the two groups never talked to each other. MpowerNPO is his answer to that gap. A structured initiative where technology education is applied, responsible, and measurable.
